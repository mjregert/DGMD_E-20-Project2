Welcome to my site!!!

As we went through the adoption process for Ayden, I tried to document all of the major events that happened, both good and bad. Here's the timeline:

September, 2002

Carey and I are looking at adopting an infant (6-9 months old). We don't have a preference as to if we get a boy or girl, so we'll probably end up with a boy. This is because there are more boys than girls available. One of the first questions people as is, what are the children from Kazakhstan like? Here are some pictures of families that have already adopted from World Partners.

Nov. 5th

We just sent off all of our paperwork which was one of the biggest steps we needed to get done. It ended up to be about 2" of paperwork total that we FedEx'd to the agency. We are now officially 'in-line' for a referral of a child, which means they're officially looking for a baby for us! Once we get a referral, we will receive a picture (or two) of the child, maybe a video if available, and the medical information. We will then take this info and send it off to a doctor that is an expert in evaluating international adoptions. If their diagnosis is positive, we then begin the process of setting up our trip to go over to Kazakhstan.

Nov. 8th - Bad News

We just found out Carey's fingerprints were rejected. This was because the person at the INS office who did her prints didn't do them well enough, so we're leaving in the morning to get them redone. Luckily, our coordinator says this won't delay us any. We're hoping to be leaving sometime in January, February, or March. We'll get about 1-2 weeks notice before we fly! Ugh! Then we'll stay in Kazakhstan for about 3 weeks to get acquainted with the baby and get all of the paperwork done. 3 of these days we'll be in Moscow. We just bought a new camcorder so we'll make sure to take lots of video.

Nov. 9th - More Bad News

INS is closed for Veterans Day. Darn it! We drove all the way down there last time and they were closed for Columbus Day, so luckily we learned to call before driving to San Antonio this time. Guess we'll go sometime next week :-( Nov. 10th We've finally decided on a name for the child. If it's a boy, we're thinking Ayden Josef Regert, and if it's a girl, Arianna Diane Regert. The middle names may change if we decide to keep their original name and use it as their middle name.

Nov. 14th

Talked with our adoption coordinator and she thinks we may not get a referral for a baby until early next year. This is because things in Kazakhstan slow down during this time because it's the time of year they celebrate their independence and then Christmas over here. This would mean that we probably won't travel until the March or April timeframe.

Nov. 16th - Good News & Really Bad News

We went down to the INS office today and had Carey's fingerprints redone. They took extra care in doing them so hopefully they will be ok this time. The INS office decided to run my(Michael) social security number again, and found out that a criminal with a bad record is using my social security number. Just what we need at this time. I had to call all three credit reporting agencies and put a fraud security alert on my accounts. We then had to put a security protection on all credit card accounts and a hold on our checking account. We're going to have to open a new checking account Monday morning (and trash the almost three boxes of check we still have - darn it!) I have to call the Federal Trade Commission as well as the Social Security office and report it. Then it's just a matter of waiting for my credit reports to be mailed to me to look them over to see what, if any, damage this person has done already.

Friday the 13th - The best day of the year!

We finally received our referral! The adoption agency has identified a child for us to adopt and emailed us a brief medical history, pictures, and a 4 minute video. We sent all of this information off to a doctor at the University of Minnesota - Department of Pediatrics and Neonatology. His evaluation was very positive and all looks good.

January 3, 2003

Carey and I had to go down to INS (Immigration and Naturalization Services) in San Antonio to check on the status of our INS/FBI clearance. Unfortunately, everyone that could help us had taken the day off to go to lunch to celebrate people's retirement. While there, we found out that they showed that Carey's fingerprints still had not passed. This was because they had put them under her maiden name originally, and the last ones we had done were put under her married name. So they made us redo her fingerprints yet a third time :-(.

January 6, 2003

We decided to drive down to San Antonio Sunday evening and stay in a hotel so that we could get to the INS office bright and early to meet with the person to discuss what the status of our clearance was as this is an important part in the process. We met with her in the morning and pulled at some heart-strings, which I think helped. By mid afternoon, she had faxed us our clearance, and had mailed it to the embassy in Moscow Russia. I guess it helps to be persistant!

January 16, 2003

Now we're waiting to get the paperwork processed in Kazakhstan and receive a call with the dates that we will travel. We then apply for our visas and book our airfare. Unfortunately, we don't know when this will happen so we're just waiting and trying to perpare everything so that we're ready when the call comes in. Kazakhstan has made some policy changes that will delay us even more. Our paperwork is now required to sit in New York for 30 days prior to being processed and sent over. Once it's sent over, it may take another 4-6 weeks to process before we receive our travel dates. This means that we'll probably not travel until mid-March or so.

February 4, 2003

Yesterday we got word that our paperwork is finally in Kazakhstan, so this means we'll be able to travel sooner than we thought. Our adoption cordinator said we are in the last stages of our adoption.

February 28, 2003

Today we received word that we will be traveling at the end of March. We'll be leaving on March 30, and returning on April 18. I can't explain how excited we are to finally get the dates. Now on to the packing and getting everything ready for the three week journey.
